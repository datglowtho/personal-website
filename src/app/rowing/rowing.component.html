Rowing has been a huge part of my life growing up. Through it I have met some incredible people and had the opportunity to push myself to achieve things I never believed I would. One day I would like to use these pages to tell the story in full, but for now please enjoy this brief summary and collection of images from my 10+ years of rowing.

After around a year messing around on my dad's rowing machine I first sat in a boat aged 10. I learnt to row at Canford Crocodiles, a weekly club for kids looking to try rowing before they went to Canford School. I stayed there for around a year and got my first taste of racing, before moving to a club closer to home, Coalporters, after choosing to go to King Edwards in Southampton instead of Canford.

Coalporters is a coastal rowing club, which meant that races with them usually consisted of battling wind and waves off a beach on the South Coast. It was there I had my crazy idea to row across the English Channel, which I saw to completion in 2012, making me the youngest person ever to row it solo at age 15, raising over Â£20,000 for Make-A-Wish in the process. Whilst fundraising at Henley Royal Regatta that year I was introduced to the junior coach at Leander Club, and a month after the cross-channel row I joined the junior squad at Leander and properly began my racing career.

During my time at Leander I won the junior singles race at the Home International Regatta 2015 for England, represented Great Britain at the Coupe de la Jeunesse 2014, achieving a bronze and a silver medal, and won a number of domestic medals at the British Junior Championships and the National Schools Regatta. Since moving to university I left Leander to row for Imperial and here I have won IM1 quads at Fours Head, won the open quads event at BUCS Head, and recently in July made it through to the quarter-finals of the Diamond Sculls event at Henley Royal Regatta, where I was knocked out (as expected) by Olympic silver medallist Cam Girdlestone.
